Why do I need to use this?

ALP uses UTM codes to track how people arrive at their website. You don't need to understand UTM codes to use this, but here's a quick example. Imagine you've placed an ad in a magazine for your product. You want to track the effectiveness of that ad. What's the easiest way to do that?

Coupons! Coupons are often little more than tracking programs. When a customer redeems a coupon, you know that he saw your product in SkyMall, because you've got a coupon code that only went to SkyMall.

UTM codes work the same way, but for links to websites. In our case, the Keycode is like a coupon code. This page will assist you in automatically assigning keycodes to links. When you fill out the form above and click "Submit," the Keycode will be automatically inserted into the link - saving you valuable time. Later, we can use this link to examine how traffic is entering our website - this helps us pinpoint high-performing emails.

Instructions for Keycode Generation

  • Keycodes are comprised of three parts. The first part is the Date. If you expect to send an email February 1, 2015, the numerical part of your keycode will be 150201 (YYMMDD).
  • The next part of the keycode will be a classification of email type. Examples: RFAR, ILN
  • Finally, and optionally, if you have included an in-line ad, it can be helpful to tag the email. For example, if the email has a CSG advertisement in it, simply add "CSG" to the end of the keycode.
  • Try it yourself: I'm sending an RFAR email on March 29, 2015. I'm planning on selling the E-Pack in this email. What's the Keycode? (150329RFAREPACK)
